# Omega Transport — Direction Plan & Storyboard

> **Concept:** The user IS the driver. Third-person GTA follow-cam. The Omega 17-seat minibus is always visible on screen — it's the hero, the guide, and the vehicle that physically takes you through the Omega world. Content appears as overlays synced to what the bus is showing you.

> **Brand colour:** Purple `#5a4bb2` — this is the primary accent across EVERYTHING. The bus stripe, signage, lighting accents, overlay text highlights, UI elements. Purple = Omega.

---

## Camera Style

- **GTA third-person follow-cam** — camera sits slightly behind and above the bus, following its movement
- Bus is ALWAYS visible, slightly left or centre of frame
- The environment tells the story — the bus drives you TO the content
- Smooth, cinematic — no jerky cuts. Every transition is a camera movement, not a hard cut
- **Purple `#5a4bb2` accent** present in every shot — bus stripe, signage, environmental lighting, neon trims on buildings, parking bay markings, etc.

## The Journey (Shot by Shot)

### ACT 1 — ARRIVAL (Home / Hero)

#### Shot 1: The Approach
- **Duration:** ~5s video (scroll: 0%–8%)
- **Camera:** Wide cinematic — bus is distant, driving toward camera on a road
- **Scene:** Early morning or golden hour. Clean road, maybe Liverpool skyline hint in the distance. The Omega-branded 17-seat minibus (white body, bold purple `#5a4bb2` horizontal stripe, "Omega Transport" text in purple on the side) approaches
- **Mood:** Anticipation. Premium. "Something's coming"
- **Overlay:** "OMEGA TRANSPORT" title in white with purple `#5a4bb2` accent glow, fades as bus gets close
- **Audio cue:** Engine hum, subtle

#### Shot 2: Get Behind the Wheel
- **Duration:** ~3s video (scroll: 8%–14%)
- **Camera:** The cinematic wide shot transitions — camera sweeps around and settles BEHIND the bus, slightly above. GTA follow-cam locked in
- **Scene:** The bus is now driving away from us on the road. We're following it. You feel like you're driving
- **Mood:** "You're in control now"
- **Overlay:** Tagline — "Quick and easy travel, **with care.**" (the "with care" in purple `#5a4bb2`) fades in and out

#### Shot 3: Feature Callouts
- **Duration:** ~5s video (scroll: 14%–26%)
- **Camera:** GTA follow-cam, bus driving straight ahead
- **Scene:** Open road, bus is cruising. Camera might subtly shift angles (slightly left, slightly right) to show different sides of the bus
- **Overlay:** Callout labels appear one by one, pointing at the bus:
  - "17 Comfortable Seats"
  - "Fully Serviced Fleet"
  - "Professional Drivers"
  - "24/7 Availability"
- **Note:** These are the selling points of THIS bus you're riding in

## Consistency Requirements

To keep all 13 shots looking like one continuous journey:

- **Brand colour:** Purple `#5a4bb2` (RGB: 90, 75, 178) — this is NON-NEGOTIABLE. It must appear in EVERY shot: on the bus, in signage, in environmental lighting, in parking bay markings, in building accents. It is the thread that ties the whole journey together
- **The bus:** White Toyota Coaster-style 17-seat minibus. Bold purple `#5a4bb2` horizontal stripe running the full length. "Omega Transport" text in purple on the side. Must look IDENTICAL in every shot
- **All fleet vehicles:** White body + purple `#5a4bb2` stripe/branding. Consistent Omega identity across the entire fleet
- **Signage & environment:** All Omega signs use purple `#5a4bb2` text on dark backgrounds. Purple LED accent lighting on buildings, lot entrance, parking bays. The purple ties the built environment to the brand
- **Lighting:** Consistent throughout. Either golden hour or bright overcast — pick one and stick with it
- **Environment style:** Clean, modern, slightly stylised (not photorealistic if that causes inconsistency). Think clean 3D render or high-quality animation
- **Camera height:** Consistent GTA follow-cam height (~3m above ground, ~5m behind bus)
- **Road/ground:** Same tarmac colour and style throughout. Purple bay markings in the parking lot
